# Campus to Market — Problem Statement

---

## Who is the customer?

People who already meditate and know their ritual — a specific sequence of silence, bells, breathwork, or chanting — but have no single tool to run it. They are consistent practitioners, not beginners. They have outgrown guided-content apps and are stitching together a timer, a music app, and sometimes a notes app just to sit down.

---

## What is the problem?

Every major meditation app (Calm, Headspace) is built to sell content. The app decides your session — you press play. If your practice doesn't fit their library, you juggle multiple tools, break your focus, and give up before you even start.

The problem is not lack of motivation. It is lack of a tool that works the way a real practice works — in ordered blocks, with custom bells, ambient sound, and transitions you designed.

**Root cause:** The industry is built on subscription content, not personal ritual composition. There is no financial incentive for these companies to let you replace their library with your own structure.

---

## How bad is it?

- **90%** of meditation app users quit within **7 days**
- Average real-world use: **2.5 sessions per month**
- Only **3–5%** of Calm or Headspace users remain after **30 days**
- Users with a fixed personal routine are **40–57% less likely** to abandon their practice
- Personalized structure improves 30-day activation by **25–40%**
